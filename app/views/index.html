Using NHS.UK design system for eligibility checker

Hypothesis A

We believe that using the NHS.UK design system for the eligibility checker will be a useful feature for every applicant who is applying for help with their health costs as it will reassure them that the eligibilty checker is a part of the wider group of NHS services. This will help users accomplish their needs more efficiently due to the fact we are now being more consistant.

The NHS Identity is one of the most cherished and recognised brands in the world. When applied correctly and consistently, it evokes exceptionally high levels of trust and reassurance.

By using NHS Business Services Authority branding for each patient facing service, we are unknowingly diluting the power of the NHS brand.

In short, by being more consistant with NHS.UK, usability and learnability will improve because we will be using similar elements which look and function in similar way.

What we changed:

  • We replicated the current eligibility checker into the NHS.UK design system.
  • After speaking to Caroline Jarrett (@cjforms), we amended the Universal Credit conditional reveal to forward off to a seperate page. This not only gets round the fact that conditional reveals are not available in the NHS.UK design system, but also helps improve usability from an accessibility point of view.

What we plan to test:

  • Do users still successfully check what help they can receive with their health costs?
  • Does usability improve because we are now being more consistant with the wider NHS?
  • As a bonus of adopting the new design system, are we now more accessible than we were previously?

Research Prototype Route A Research Prototype Route B

Research Details

The purpose of this round of UR is to compare user feedback about two proposed wording changes for Universal Credit, part of the User Journey for Eligibility Checker. Participants who are recipients of Universal Credit will complete three user tasks: 'As Is', followed by a subsequent A/B testing component to compare the two proposed sets of wording, so that we can understand which version is most beneficial for our users.
