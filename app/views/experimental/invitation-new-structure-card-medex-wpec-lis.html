Recommended for you

Medical exemption certificate

Because you have a long-term medical condition, you can apply for a medical exemption certificate which entitles you to free prescriptions.

What it includes

  • free NHS prescriptions
  • free sight tests
    because you have diabetes

How to get your help

  • Ask your GP practice for an FP92A form to apply for a medical exemption certificate.
  • Contact your local optician to book a free sight test. It does not cover the cost of glasses or contact lenses.

War pension exemption certificate

This certificate gives you help for treatment related to your accepted disablement, if you have an illness or injury caused by service in the armed forces.

What it includes

  • free NHS prescriptions
  • free NHS wigs and fabric supports

Important: Warning

Your certificate only covers prescription for your accepted disablement. If you're not sure, contact Veterans UK (opens in new tab) before you claim.

How to get your help

If you have an illness or injury caused by service in the armed forces, you need to contact Veterans UK (opens in new tab) to find out about the help you are entitled to.

Find out more about war pension exemption certificate (opens in new tab)

Also available

NHS Low Income Scheme

You may get help with NHS costs based on your circumstances.

Your household income will be taken into account as well as your council tax and housing costs.

You don't have to be claiming benefits to apply.

Any help you get also covers your partner and children under 19.

Full list of help you may get

You may get help paying for:

  • NHS prescriptions.
  • NHS dental treatment.
  • sight tests.
  • new glasses or contact lenses.
  • repairing or replacing your glasses or contact lenses.
  • NHS wigs and fabric supports.
  • travel for NHS treatment.

Find out more about NHS Low Income Scheme (opens in new tab)
